# Economics of Transformative AI and Autonomous Agents

Welcome to the course website for the Spring 2026 reading group on the economics of AI. This bi-weekly course explores how autonomous agents and foundation models are reshaping firms, pricing, and market structures.

--

## Course Overview

Recent advances in AI—specifically autonomous agents and large foundation models—are transforming core economic questions. AI agents can search, negotiate, and transact on behalf of users, potentially reducing transaction costs while introducing new frictions like congestion and strategic behavior. 

This course aims to develop a rigorous understanding of these transformations and identify open research directions at the intersection of **Computer Science, Economics, and Operations Research.**

### Format & Audience
- **Meetings:** Every two weeks (1.5 hours).
- **Style:** Discussion-based, centered on recent research papers.
- **Target Audience:** PhD students and advanced researchers in CS, Economics, OR, and AI Policy.

---

## Learning Goals

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:
* Formulate research questions regarding the **economics of AI agents**.
* Model **pricing and market design** for AI-mediated services.
* Analyze the equilibrium effects of **agent-led platforms**.
* Identify publishable research directions in this rapidly evolving field.

---

## Course Themes

The curriculum is organized around six interconnected themes:

| Theme | Focus Areas |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Foundations** | Derived demand for agentic decision-making, alignment, and multi-agent equilibrium. |
| **2. Market Design** | Mechanism design for autonomous transacting, search/matching, and bargaining protocols. |
| **3. Pricing & Power** | AI as an intermediate input, nonlinear pricing, and bottlenecks in compute/models. |
| **4. Firms & Knowledge** | Boundaries of the firm, decision rights, and the codification of tacit knowledge. |
| **5. Labor & Growth** | "Genius on demand," cognitive labor allocation, and compute as a growth driver. |
| **6. Ecosystems** | AI-assisted decision-making, information quality, and Behavioral Economics 2.0. |
