# Economics of Transformative AI and Autonomous Agents

Welcome to the **Spring 2026 Reading Seminar**. This bi-weekly group explores how autonomous agents and foundation models are reshaping firms, pricing, and market structures.

---

## Seminar Overview

Recent advances in AI—specifically autonomous agents and large foundation models—are transforming core economic questions. AI agents can search, negotiate, and transact on behalf of users, potentially reducing transaction costs while introducing new frictions like congestion and strategic behavior. 

This seminar aims to bridge **Computer Science, Economics, and Operations Research** to identify open research directions in this rapidly evolving field.

### Format & Audience
- **Meetings:** Every two weeks (1.5 hours).
- **Style:** Discussion-led. Each session pairs a "Foundational" theory with a "Novel Frontier" paper.
- **Target Audience:** PhD students and researchers in CS, Economics, OR, and AI Policy.

---

## Discussion Objectives

Our sessions are designed to help participants:
* **Bridge Disciplines:** Map agentic CS architectures (like LLM-based agents) onto formal economic equilibrium models.
* **Identify Research Gaps:** Pinpoint areas where current mechanism design fails to account for autonomous, high-speed agentic behavior.
* **Analyze Firm Dynamics:** Evaluate how delegation to AI shifts decision rights and the "make-vs-buy" boundary for companies.
* **Anticipate Market Shifts:** Discuss the implications of "Genius on Demand" and compute-driven growth models.
